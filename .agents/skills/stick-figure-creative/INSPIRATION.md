# Stick Figure / Cartoon Animation — Channel Inspiration Catalog

> Research compiled April 2026. Focus: channels building stick-figure or simple-cartoon content at scale, with lessons for a Vietnamese adaptation.
> Subscriber numbers are approximate and sourced from web search (Wikipedia, Wikitubia, Social Blade, Fandom). Treat as directional, not exact.

## Global channels

### 1. Alan Becker
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@alanbecker
- **Subscribers:** ~25M (2026)
- **Genre:** Stick figure fight / meta animation
- **Art style:** Clean black stick figures on white/desktop backgrounds. No faces, no color on the figures themselves — all expression is conveyed through posture and motion. Environment provides the color (desktop UI, Minecraft world, Animator's interface).
- **Content series they built:**
  - **Animator vs. Animation** (flagship, since 2006) — a stick figure escapes the animator's canvas and fights back. Episodes I–V.
  - **Animation vs. Minecraft** — stick figures invade Minecraft; most-viewed series on the channel.
  - **Animation vs. Math / Physics / Geometry / YouTube / League of Legends** — stick figures explore an abstract or real system. "Animation vs Math" turned into a viral edu-hit.
- **Typical video format:** 8–20 min, no dialogue, pure visual storytelling. Hook: a single stick figure appears and something immediately goes wrong. Outro: cliffhanger or peaceful reset.
- **Voice/narrator style:** None — 100% silent / SFX + music. Language-agnostic = global reach.
- **Why they work:**
  1. Zero language barrier — one video = 200+ countries.
  2. Hyper-kinetic choreography — every frame earns its place.
  3. "Meta" premise (the animator is a character too) creates infinite story fuel.
- **Lessons for VN:** Silent action sequences travel. A recurring "stick figure explores system X" template is printable. Don't underestimate the power of *no words*.

### 2. TheOdd1sOut (Robert Rallison)
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@TheOdd1sOut
- **Subscribers:** ~18M
- **Genre:** Storytime personal anecdote
- **Art style:** Marshmallow-shaped cartoon body, bean-shaped head, dot eyes, minimal limbs. Flat color, thick outline, Flash/ToonBoom style. Reads instantly on thumbnails.
- **Content series:**
  - **"Life of a..."** — "Life of a YouTuber", "Life of a Pigeon", etc.
  - **Sooubway** — his part-time job at Subway, became a mini-franchise.
  - **Reading comments** — low-effort filler series.

- **Voice:** Own voice, nasally, casual — signature delivery.
- **Why they work:** Relatable "I worked at X / I was weird in school" stories + distinctive voice + consistent character design.
- **Lessons for VN:** Build ONE character that's instantly recognizable in 1x1 thumbnail. Mine your own job/school/family stories — authenticity scales.

## Pattern analysis

### Content series types (ranked by prevalence in successful channels)
1. **Personal storytime anecdote** — ~60% of channels (TheOdd1sOut, Jaiden, Domics, Emirichu, SomeThingElseYT, Ice Cream Sandwich). Most crowded lane.
2. **Deadpan explainer / life guide** — ~20% (Casually Explained, CGP Grey). Least competitive, highest writing demand.
3. **Edutainment / history** — ~15% (Sam O'Nella, Kurzgesagt). Niche-knowledge moat.
4. **Serialized fiction / D&D / meta-animation** — ~10% (Alan Becker, Dingo Doodles). Highest retention, highest effort.
5. **Philosophical essay** — small but loyal (Exurb1a). Perfect fit for VN spiritual/Tịnh Đạo angle.

### Video format patterns
- **Duration:** 4–8 min is the sweet spot. Alan Becker and Dingo Doodles go 10–20+. Shorts/TikTok go 30–90 sec.
- **Hook:** First 3 seconds must show the character + set the situation. Words on screen ("I almost died at Subway") in bold.
- **Structure:** Hook → setup → escalation (3 beats minimum) → callback punchline / philosophical beat.
- **Outro:** Channel bumper + "see you next time" in-character.

### Thumbnail patterns
- 1 character face, huge, exaggerated expression.
- 3–5 bold words, usually in yellow/red.
- Background is a single color or a single recognizable object.
- No face reveal — character IS the brand.

### Character design patterns
- **Head:** Round or bean-shaped, 40–50% of total body height (chibi proportion).
- **Body:** Minimal — no realistic anatomy. Bean, blob, or stick.
- **Eyes:** Dots, lines, or simple ovals. Max expression with min geometry.
- **Color:** ONE signature color per character (Jaiden = pink hoodie, Odd1sOut = yellow). Recognizable in 1-cm thumbnail.
- **Props:** ONE recurring prop (Sam O'Nella's long hair, CGP Grey's tie, Bút Chì's pencil body).

### Voice patterns
- Own voice in 90% of channels. Hired VO only for Kurzgesagt-tier institutional channels.
- Deadpan/monotone is a valid signature (Casually Explained).
- TTS is considered low-effort and hurts retention on long-form — but works on TikTok shorts.

## Recommended series to steal/adapt for VN channel

### 1. "Chuyện Cũ Kể Lại" (Old Stories Retold)
- **Based on:** Sam O'Nella Academy + Exurb1a
- **Format:** 5–8 min, stick figure narrator retells weird Vietnamese historical events, folk tales, or làng cười gossip with dark humor.
- **5 topic ideas:** Trạng Quỳnh's weirdest prank; the real story behind Bánh Chưng Bánh Dày; why Tết lì xì started; the curse of the Nguyễn dynasty's tombs; what ancient Vietnamese actually ate.
- **Why viral in VN:** Historical edutainment is under-served in VN. Teenagers hate textbooks but love gossip.
- **Effort:** Med — writing-heavy, art-light.

### 2. "Giải Thích Kiểu Lười" (Casually Explained VN)
- **Based on:** Casually Explained
- **Format:** 3–5 min deadpan stick-figure guides to everyday VN life.
- **5 topics:** How to survive Tết with mẹ; an incomplete guide to trà đá vỉa hè; how to order at a quán nhậu; an incomplete guide to xe ôm công nghệ; how to pretend you understand Phật pháp.
- **Why viral in VN:** Script-driven, low art budget, instantly relatable. Nobody in VN is doing this voice yet.
- **Effort:** Low — the whole moat is the writing.

### 3. "Tịnh Đạo Kể Chuyện" (already in progress — sharpen it)
- **Based on:** Exurb1a
- **Format:** 4–7 min philosophical stick-figure essay, VN Buddhist/Taoist angle.
- **5 topics:** Vô thường and why your ex left; karma debugging; the monk and the GenZ; three questions the Buddha never answered; why suffering is the feature, not the bug.
- **Why viral in VN:** VN audience is spiritually curious but bored of traditional dharma delivery. Stick figure + philosophy is underused.
- **Effort:** Med — writing + atmosphere.

### 4. "Người Que vs ___" (Stick Figure VS Series)
- **Based on:** Alan Becker
- **Format:** 2–4 min silent action — stick figure fights/explores a VN cultural system. No voice, music only.
- **5 topics:** Người Que vs Bát Phở; Người Que vs Xe Máy Sài Gòn; Người Que vs Bộ Bài Tú Lơ Khơ; Người Que vs Bàn Nhậu; Người Que vs Đám Cưới.
- **Why viral in VN:** Silent = exportable. Visual comedy about VN culture travels internationally to diaspora.
- **Effort:** High — animation-heavy. Save this for the #1 hero series.

### 5. "Chuyện Nhà Việt" (Life of a Vietnamese)
- **Based on:** TheOdd1sOut / Domics
- **Format:** 6–10 min storytime, personal VN family/school anecdotes.
- **5 topics:** The time mẹ found my diary; surviving thi đại học; why I lied about dating at Tết; the ghost story bà kể me as a kid; what it's like to be the con lớn.
- **Why viral in VN:** Deeply relatable, nostalgia-driven, shareable to family group chats.
- **Effort:** Med.

### 6. "Hỏi Ngu Đáp Khôn" (Dumb Questions, Smart Answers)
- **Based on:** CGP Grey + Casually Explained
- **Format:** 3–5 min explainer answering a stupid question seriously with stick figures and diagrams.
- **5 topics:** Why are Vietnamese names three words?; what happens to your xe máy after it dies?; why do bún and phở use different noodles?; who decides the Tết date?; why is HCMC traffic like that?
- **Effort:** Low-Med.

### 7. "Serial Người Que" (Stick Figure D&D / Serial)
- **Based on:** Dingo Doodles
- **Format:** 10–15 min serialized adventure with recurring cast of 4–6 characters. Think a VN folk-tale universe (Sơn Tinh Thủy Tinh remixed, or a Tấm Cám detective AU).
- **Effort:** Very high. Save for after channel has 100K subs.

### 8. "Thầy Tu GenZ" (Monk Meets GenZ)
- **Based on:** Daniel Thrasher's archetype skits, but animated.
- **Format:** 2–4 min skit, fixed characters (monk, GenZ kid, mẹ, boss) in conversation. Philosophical comedy.
- **Effort:** Low — one background, same characters every episode.

| 2 | Chuyện Nhà Việt #1 (mẹ found my diary) | Chuyện Cũ Kể Lại #1 (Trạng Quỳnh) | Thầy Tu GenZ #1 |

## Key takeaways (TL;DR)

1. **Writing > art.** Casually Explained proves crude visuals + god-tier script beats polished art + average script. For a VN channel starting out, INVEST in the script.
2. **One character = one brand.** Every successful channel has ONE recognizable mascot in 1-cm thumbnail. Design yours before episode 1 and never change it.
3. **VN adult stick-figure is a blue ocean.** The VN animation market on YouTube is dominated by kids content. There is no Vietnamese Casually Explained, no Vietnamese Sam O'Nella, no Vietnamese Exurb1a. That's the opening.
4. **Series > one-offs.** Every giant channel has 2–3 named recurring series. Build named series from day one.
5. **Silence scales globally.** Alan Becker's no-dialogue format hits every country. One silent hero series per month is cheap international insurance.
